Why Join the Community?

Linux is built on the philosophy of open collaboration. Whether you are troubleshooting a broken GRUB bootloader, trying to configure your first tiling window manager, or looking for recommendations on the best open-source software, you shouldn't have to do it alone.

By joining JR Nation, you gain access to a network of like-minded users ranging from complete beginners to advanced system administrators.

💬 The Official Discord Server: The fastest way to get help or share your desktop ricing setups is by joining our active Discord community.

How to Contribute to The Hub

Because we are dedicated to providing the most accurate and up-to-date Linux documentation, we rely on community testing and feedback. Since this site is statically generated, contributing is easy:

  • Found a typo or a broken command? Let us know in the community channels so we can patch the documentation.
  • Wrote a great bash script? Share it with us! We regularly feature user-submitted scripts and configurations in our Pro User section.
  • Hardware testing: Help us build our hardware compatibility lists by reporting how different distros perform on your specific laptop or PC build.

Community Guidelines

We strictly enforce a "No Elitism" policy. The Linux community has a notorious reputation for telling new users to "Just Read The Manual" (RTFM). At JR Nation, we do things differently.

  • Be Patient: Everyone was a noob once. Answer basic questions with respect.
  • Be Constructive: If you are pointing out a flaw in someone's code or configuration, explain why it is flawed and offer a solution.
  • No Gatekeeping: Whether someone is using Ubuntu with a graphical interface or compiling Arch from scratch, they are a valued member of this hub.
