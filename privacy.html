The Boring (But Legally Required) Privacy Stuff.

Last updated: Right before we launched.

1. The Honest Truth

Look, I am just a guy who learned Linux because I lived with an obsessed homie. I do not care about stealing your personal data. I don't care what your real name is. But to keep these servers running, the domain paid for, and the terminal engine alive, I had to put some ads on here to pay the bills.

2. The Google Overlords & Cookies

Google pays the server bills, so Google gets to put cookies on your browser. Third-party vendors (yeah, mostly Google) use cookies to serve you ads based on your prior visits to JR Nation or other sites on the internet.

It’s how the modern web works. If you hate personalized ads and want them to stop tracking your stuff, you can literally tell them to back off by visiting Google's Ad Settings.

3. Server Logs (Like Every Site Since 1995)

Like literally every website ever created, our hosting provider collects basic log files. This means when you drop in, the server jots down your IP address, browser type, and what time you showed up. It’s not a secret file on you; it’s just the server making sure it’s talking to a real computer and not a spam bot.

4. What Do I Actually Do With Your Data?

Nothing. I don't sell it. I don't have a creepy corporate marketing team analyzing your habits. The only thing I care about is checking the dashboard to see how many people are using the terminal so I know if the site is crashing.

5. The Agreement

By using JR Nation, you consent to this policy. If you don't like it, you can always close the tab, boot up a bare-metal Arch Linux machine, and go read a 50-page manual instead. Your call.
